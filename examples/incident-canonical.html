Public summary

Between 14:00 and 14:35 UTC on 2026-05-21, a subset of user chat sessions silently failed to produce a response. The chat front end displayed the "thinking" indicator but no message arrived. The on-call team identified an unhandled timeout in the SSE proxy layer, deployed a mitigation at 14:18, and confirmed full recovery at 14:35. No data was lost; all affected sessions can be retried.

Root cause & contributing factors

The streaming SSE proxy in Gateway Service wraps the Agent Service's response stream. The wrapper had no per-message timeout, relying on the upstream Agent Service to close the connection. When the Agent Service hit a transient burst of load and stalled (rather than erroring), the SSE proxy held connections open indefinitely, starving the worker pool. Three contributing factors:

  1. Missing timeout: the SSE generator used async for chunk in upstream: without an outer asyncio.wait_for.
  2. Upstream stall behavior: Agent Service's agent loop, under transient load, paused rather than failing fast.
  3. Detection blind spot: our existing alerts watched p95 latency but not connection accumulation; the worker-pool exhaustion was caught only after user-facing degradation.

Lessons learned

  • What went well: mitigation in 18 minutes; clear ownership; runbook for SSE proxy was current.
  • What did not: detection delayed by 4 minutes because the alert watched latency rather than connection accumulation.
  • What we'd do differently: include connection-pool saturation in the chat-resilience scenario suite so similar gaps surface in CI rather than in production.
